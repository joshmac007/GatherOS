Privacy policy

Last updated: July 21, 2026

GatherLocal is a local-first desktop app. Its library lives under ~/Library/Application Support/GatherLocal/. GatherLocal does not collect analytics or telemetry and does not operate a server that stores your library.

Optional ChatGPT Codex features

If you connect ChatGPT, OAuth tokens are stored as ciphertext encrypted by Electron safeStorage, backed by macOS Keychain. Tokens are sent only to auth.openai.com for authentication and chatgpt.com for enabled Codex requests. Relevant images and text are sent directly to OpenAI when those features are enabled.

Credentials are excluded from GatherLocal library exports and snapshots, although system-level backups may contain encrypted vault ciphertext. Log out in Settings → Local AI to remove saved ChatGPT credentials and cancel active AI requests.

GatherLocal stores aggregate AI request outcomes and token counts in ai-usage.json on this Mac so Settings can show observed usage. This ledger does not contain prompts, images, responses, or credentials, and it does not represent remaining ChatGPT plan allowance.

The GatherLocal browser extension does not sell captured data. It sends captures only to the GatherLocal desktop application running on your computer, except for service-specific requests described below.

What the extension handles

The extension passes captured data to the GatherLocal desktop application running locally on your own machine, via Chrome’s native messaging protocol. Depending on how you save:

  • Saving images and pages — when you right-click an image and choose “Save to GatherLocal”, or use the in-page capture panel to capture the page, a region, the full page, or its link: the image or capture, plus the URL and title of the page you were viewing.
  • Saving your X (Twitter) bookmarks — when you bookmark a tweet on x.com, the extension reads that tweet’s content (its text, author name and handle, and any image or video URLs) and forwards it to the desktop app. To pick up bookmarks you made on other devices, it also re-issues your own bookmarks request to x.com in the background, using your existing x.com session cookie to authenticate it.

This data goes only to the GatherLocal desktop application on your computer and to x.com itself — your own authenticated request, which never leaves your browser session. It is used solely to store the content in your local library, with the source URL preserved as a breadcrumb.

What the extension stores

So your X bookmark sync doesn’t import the same tweet twice, the extension keeps a small amount of bookkeeping data locally in chrome.storage.local — the IDs of tweets already imported, plus the template of your own bookmarks request used to refresh them. This stays on your machine. The extension maintains no remote server and includes no analytics or telemetry.

Permissions in use

  • contextMenus — to register the “Save to GatherLocal” right-click menu item.
  • nativeMessaging — to communicate with the GatherLocal desktop application installed on the same machine.
  • scripting / activeTab — to inject the in-page capture panel into the current tab when you click the toolbar icon.
  • storage — to remember which X bookmarks have already been imported.
  • alarms — to periodically check for newly added X bookmarks in the background.
  • cookies — to read your existing x.com session cookie so your own bookmarks request can be re-issued to x.com.
  • notifications — to show a single confirmation toast after a save.
  • Host access to x.com / twitter.com — to detect when you bookmark a tweet and read its content for saving.

Third parties

The extension has no GatherLocal server. It communicates with the GatherLocal desktop application on your computer and with x.com when re-issuing your own bookmarks request using your existing session. Optional desktop AI transfers are described above.
